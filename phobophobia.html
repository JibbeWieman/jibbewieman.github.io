Phobophobia is my first university project, where I served as Lead Programmer and Level Designer.
I collaborated with a team of five other students to bring our vision to life. The game is about overcoming fears and solving puzzles in a story-driven environment. I worked on this project from December 2023 to February 2024, dedicating about 7-8 weeks to its development. My key contributions included programming player movement, developing the camera system, coding the lighting, designing room mechanics, creating puzzles, implementing farming elements, and setting up warps. During this project, I learned coding principles, GML, and game design.


My Contributions

Implemented sound effects, background music, and audio triggers for a more immersive experience.

Designed and implemented a dynamic camera system, including smooth transitions and player tracking.

Handled dialogue display and interactions, ensuring seamless text presentation and progression.

Developed a farming mechanic that allows players to plant, grow, and harvest crops.

Implemented a lighting system with dynamic effects to enhance the game's atmosphere.

Designed and refined the player movement system, including walking, running, and interactions.

Created a mechanic allowing objects to be pushed for puzzles and environmental interaction.

Developed logic for various in-game puzzles, ensuring engaging and solvable challenges.

Implemented a seamless room transition system, managing loading and visual continuity.
